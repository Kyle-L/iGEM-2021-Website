Prinicipal Investigator and Instructors

Xin Wang

| Assistant Professor of Microbiology

Fun Fact: I used to love novels but now I can only read non-fictions.

Xin Wang is an assistant professor of microbiology and serves as the primary principal investigator on Miami Universitys iGEM team. Dr. Wang has been with Miami University since 2017 after completing a post-doctoral fellowship at Texas A&M University and receiving his doctorate from the University of Hawaii at Manoa in 2013. He is an expert in metabolic engineering, synthetic biology, and the metabolism of microbes. His research investigates cyanobacterial metabolisms to eventually rewire the central metabolism to sustain cell growth and meet engineering goals such as biofuel production. Aside from his own lab research, he inspired the formation of this team.

Luis Actis

| Professor and Chair of Microbiology

Fun Fact: Actis is originally from Crdoba, Argentina.

Luis Actis serves as the secondary principal investigator on Miami Universitys iGEM team. After receiving his Ph.D. from the National University of Crdoba in Crdoba, Argentina, Dr. Actis has served as a professor of and the chair of Miami Universitys microbiology department. His current research centers on the genetic, molecular and functional analyses of some of the factors responsible for the ability of the bacterium Acinetobacter baumannii to persist in medical environments and cause serious infections in humans.

Zhenxiong "Shaun" Jiangs

| PhD Student in Computational Microbiology

Fun Fact: I am happy to be back in the game even though I'm in a different role!

Zhenxiong "Shaun" Jiang is a computational microbiology PhD student and is serving as the instructor for the team. Shaun served as a team lead for his iGEM team NJAU_China in 2014 and is now using his experience to help guide the Miami University team. He is mainly responsible for the instruction of the computational modeling team, but he also constantly shares with the entire team reminders and helpful tips.
